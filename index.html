Research

Rumour Evolution Mining

Rumour Evolution Mining aims at understanding how rumours in diverse domains evolve through social media, and predicting their behaviour in real time. Once a rumour draws the public's attention, large-scale user-generated content regarding the rumour including truth and false information are rapidly produced and propagates throughout social media even before mainstream media publish articles. Therefore, it is important to be able to identify particular moments of which journalists should take notice before the dissemination of rumours including disinformation has a catastrophic impact on the public. Methods for automatically predicting noteworthy moments likely to be of interest to journalists during rumour evolution are currently being developed. This project combines large-scale temporal data and outlier analytics. Moreover, the incorporation of simple but effective and efficient natural language processing will enhance the performance of the proposed methods.
